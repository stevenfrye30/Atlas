# 2025-05-08: Cadence disciplines articulated

## Decision

Articulate the operational disciplines for how editorial work proceeds across time. The new document `stewardship/cadence.md` covers pacing, pauses, entity selection, gestation, revision restraint, expansion restraint, early warning signs of production drift, atmosphere preservation, and refusal of production pressure.

## Reasoning

The prior phase clarified that carefully made entities are the substance of continuity work. This clarity creates a specific psychological pressure: the steward feels that more entities means more contribution. The pressure is sincere and corrosive.

The architecture's commitment to slow growth was previously articulated structurally (in `disciplines.md`) but not operationalized at the level of what stewardship cadence actually looks like across years. Cadence operates at a different register than the disciplines: it is about the rhythm of work, the relation between productive and pause periods, the recognition of when production drift has begun.

Without cadence articulation, two failure modes are likely:

- **Production drift.** Stewardship gradually accelerates as infrastructure stabilizes; entities are produced at increasing rate; atmospheric register drifts; editorial restraint thins; the architecture becomes a production system without anyone deciding it should.
- **Performative slowness.** Stewardship overcorrects toward visible restraint; pauses become performance; the discipline of "not yet" becomes ceremonial hesitation rather than honest deferral.

The cadence document attempts to articulate the path between these failures: real pauses serving real work, real productive periods when warranted, real attention to whether the rhythm is matching the labor's actual demands.

## Relationship to other stewardship documents

- `disciplines.md` — structural commitments (no analytics, no optimization, no platformization). Foundational and stable.
- `editorial-constitution.md` — editorial-craft disciplines (commentary, translation, adjacency, ontology, omission). How entities are made.
- `apprenticeship.md` — steward formation. How new stewards develop.
- `cadence.md` (new) — temporal disciplines. How editorial work proceeds across years.
- `editorial-conventions.md` — working conventions for specific editorial choices.
- `relationship-vocabulary.md` — vocabulary reference.
- `workflow.md` — operational workflow for adding/revising entities.

The four substantive documents (disciplines, constitution, apprenticeship, cadence) cover what the architecture is committed to, how its editorial work is performed, how its stewards are formed, and how its work proceeds across time. Together they articulate the practice; none alone is sufficient.

## What this phase did not do

No new entities were added. No infrastructure was modified. The Death region remains at 24 entities; the Breath region at 2.

The constitution itself was not revised. The apprenticeship structure was not revised. The phase produced one new document (cadence.md) and substantial discursive analysis that exists in stewardship working memory.

## Open questions

- The relationship between cadence disciplines and stewardship plurality. The cadence disciplines mostly assume single-steward decision-making about pace; how cadence operates when multiple stewards have different rhythm preferences is not directly addressed.
- The boundary between productive periods (legitimate) and production drift (corrupting). The cadence document gives warning signs but not a sharp line; the line is interpretive, requiring stewardship judgment.
- The application of cadence disciplines to apprenticeship-period stewards. New apprentices may need to produce more entities than mature stewardship requires, simply to develop the editorial sensibility through doing the work. How cadence applies to apprentices versus to formed stewards is not articulated.

These are stewardship questions for ongoing review.
