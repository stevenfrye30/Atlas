# 2025-05-08: Editorial constitution articulated

## Decision

Articulate the editorial disciplines that have emerged from the prior phases of work into a formal stewardship document — `stewardship/editorial-constitution.md`. The constitution covers fourteen disciplinary areas: material primacy, civilizational specificity, editorial restraint, commentary boundaries, translation honesty, adjacency discipline, ontology restraint, omission as editorial act, provenance visibility, boundedness, revisability, anti-totalization, architectural invisibility, and stewardship as practice.

## Reasoning

The prior phases (initial skeleton, first real corpus, deepening of the Death region) revealed that the architecture's deepest pressures are no longer technical but editorial. Several recurring decisions cannot be solved through implementation:

- When does commentary become flattening?
- When should silence remain?
- When does adjacency become typological flattening?
- How should multiple translations be presented without resolution?
- When should an entity remain thin?
- How should the architecture's own commitments avoid colonizing entity prose?

These are constitutional editorial questions. They are not solvable by code; they require articulated stewardship judgment. The constitution articulates the judgments that the prior editorial work has actually made, so that future stewards have access to the disciplines as articulated practice rather than tacit knowledge.

The articulation is grounded in specific cases. Each discipline points to actual editorial moments where honoring or violating it produced visible consequences. The constitution is operational; it is not a manifesto.

## What the constitution does and does not do

It documents:
- The disciplines in operational form, with conditions for application.
- The cases that revealed each discipline.
- The relationship between the constitution and existing stewardship documents (`disciplines.md` for structural commitments, this constitution for editorial-craft commitments).

It does not:
- Guarantee good editorial work. Following the disciplines without inhabited sensibility produces compliance, not stewardship.
- Anticipate every editorial situation. The architecture's revisability discipline applies to the constitution itself.
- Replace stewardship apprenticeship. The disciplines transmit fully only through working alongside stewards who have inhabited them.

## Relationship to existing documents

The existing `disciplines.md` covers structural commitments — no analytics, no optimization, no platform mechanics, anti-totalization, slow growth, long duration. These remain foundational and are not revised by this phase.

The new `editorial-constitution.md` covers the editorial-craft disciplines that have emerged from doing the work — how commentary is bounded, how translation is handled, how adjacency is decided, when entities are revised. The two documents are complementary; neither subsumes the other.

`relationship-vocabulary.md` and `editorial-conventions.md` continue to serve as more detailed working references for specific aspects of the work; they are subordinate to the constitution but more granular.

## What this phase did not do

The architecture did not gain new entities. The Death region remains at 22 entities; the Breath region at 2.

The build script and templates were not modified. The custom YAML parser was not extended. No new infrastructure was added.

The phase produced one new file (the constitution), one new decision record (this file), and no entity changes. By the metric of visible output, the phase produced little. By the metric of stewardship infrastructure, it produced something significant: the articulation of the disciplines that govern future editorial work.

## Open questions remaining

Several questions the constitution flags as unresolved:

- Whether the structurally_similar triangle among the three terms (*maraṇa* ↔ *thanátos* ↔ *sǐ*) should be reconsidered, given that the typological resemblance is uneven.
- Whether the concept-anchor model is the right organizational structure for regions, given the thinness of *concept-death* relative to the substantive entities.
- How the architecture should handle deeply different forms of continuity beyond the textual mode (oral tradition, embodied practice, ritual transmission) when those become editorially relevant.

These are stewardship questions for ongoing review.
