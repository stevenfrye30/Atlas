# 2025-05-08: Apprenticeship structure articulated

## Decision

Articulate the apprenticeship structure that complements the editorial constitution. The new document `stewardship/apprenticeship.md` provides twenty operational recommendations covering onboarding, apprenticeship structure, review practices, revision discussion, constitutional usage, editorial collaboration, and protection against both drift and rigidity.

## Reasoning

The simulated apprenticeship in this phase revealed that the editorial constitution is necessary but not sufficient for stewardship transmission. Several disciplines — editorial restraint, the line between commentary that articulates structure and commentary that articulates meaning, the threshold judgments about adjacency inclusion, the inhabited register of the architecture's prose — transmit poorly through documents alone. They require shared work over months and years.

Without an articulated apprenticeship structure, two failure modes are likely:

- **Drift.** New stewards interpret disciplines through their own training without dialogical correction. The architecture gradually becomes whatever the new stewardship brings.
- **Rigidity.** New stewards treat the constitution as a rule book. Editorial work that requires judgment freezes into mechanical compliance.

The apprenticeship structure addresses both. It is small-scale, realistic, non-institutional. It assumes the architecture is one small site of continuity work, not a platform or institution.

## What this phase did not do

The phase produced no new entities. The Death region remains at 22 entities; the Breath region at 2.

The build script and templates were not modified. The constitution itself was not revised. No infrastructure was added.

The phase produced one new file (apprenticeship.md), one decision record (this file), and substantial discursive analysis that exists in stewardship working memory but not in the source. By visible-output measures, the phase produced little.

By stewardship-infrastructure measures, the phase articulated what the architecture's continuity actually requires across stewardship generations. Without this articulation, the constitution alone would have been treated as the transmission mechanism — and the simulation showed it is not.

## What was learned about the constitution itself

The simulation surfaced several constitutional sections that may be over-articulated or insufficiently operational:

- The 100-400 word typical-length guideline (III) may produce judgment-substitution rather than judgment-support.
- The Notes section's purpose list (IV.4) is partial; some legitimate uses are not captured.
- The adjacency discipline (VI) may produce fear of adjacency rather than discernment.
- The ontology restraint (VII) may produce stewards who never extend the ontology when extension is warranted.
- The architectural invisibility discipline (XIII) lacks crisp operational distinction between justified and narcissistic meta-commentary.

These are not constitutional defects requiring immediate revision. They are observations about where the constitution's articulation is most likely to produce difficulty for apprentices. Future stewardship may revise the constitution to address them; in the meantime, apprenticeship dialogue covers what the document does not.

## Open questions

- The relationship between the apprenticeship structure and current single-steward operation. The architecture currently operates with one principal steward; the recommendations assume stewardship plurality from the start. The transition from one steward to plural stewardship is itself a stewardship-formation event that the document does not directly address.
- Whether annual constitutional review (recommendation 20) is the right rhythm. Different rhythms — quarterly, biennial, on-demand — may serve better. This may be revised after the first review cycle.
- How the apprenticeship structure handles transition between stewardship cohorts (when current stewardship retires or steps back). The recommendations cover apprenticeship; they do not directly cover succession at scale.
