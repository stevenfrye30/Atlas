# Disciplines

These are the operational disciplines under which the prototype is built and maintained. They are not aspirational — they are constitutive. Violating them does not produce a worse version of the environment; it produces a categorically different and corrupted thing.

The articulation here is provisional. Stewards may refine these disciplines as operational reality reveals where they need refinement. Refinements are committed with reasoning in `decisions/`.

## Editorial restraint

Each entity is held at the depth its civilizational specificity warrants. The prose stops where the editorial work has actually been done, not where some standard length suggests it should stop. Brevity is not minimalism; it is the consequence of refusing to say more than the work supports.

## Civilizational specificity

Source-language terms remain in their source languages. Prāṇa stays prāṇa. Maraṇa stays maraṇa. Glosses gesture toward what the term does without claiming to translate it. The gloss is not equivalence; it is a small bridge for readers who do not yet hold the term.

Cross-civilizational resemblances are noted as typological without being collapsed into genealogical claims. *Structurally_similar* is a relationship type; *equivalent* is not.

## Adjacency sparseness

Each entity has few adjacencies. Three to six is typical. The discipline at each candidate adjacency is to ask whether this relation rises to the level of editorial inclusion — whether following this adjacency would actually deepen a reader's engagement. The default answer is no.

Saturation defeats adjacency. A graph in which every related entity is linked to every plausibly related entity has stopped performing editorial judgment.

## Anti-totalization

The architecture is partial by structural commitment. There is more outside it than inside it. Deferred entities, unwritten regions, asymmetric coverage are not deficiencies to be repaired; they are structural features.

Completeness is not a goal. Coverage is not a goal. What is rendered is rendered with care; what is not is not promised.

## No analytics, no behavioral data

The environment does not know its readers. No tracking, no analytics, no profiles, no behavioral data. The reader is anonymous to the system; the system is the same for every reader. This is a structural commitment, not a privacy preference.

## No optimization

Nothing about the environment is optimized against any metric. There is no engagement metric, no retention metric, no growth metric, no completion metric. Stewardship work is evaluated qualitatively against these disciplines, not against measurable outcomes.

## No platform mechanics

No comments, no community features, no user contributions, no social visibility, no shared anything. The environment is between reader and material. Community happens elsewhere if it happens.

## Stewardship plurality

The work is performed by a small group. No single steward is irreplaceable; no founder owns the work. Editorial decisions are reviewable; major changes are discussed; the disciplines themselves are revisable.

## Slow growth

New material appears at the rhythm of substantive editorial work. There is no publishing schedule, no content calendar, no throughput target. Some months bring new entities; some do not.

## Long duration

The environment is built for decades, not years. Technical choices privilege durability over novelty. Stable URLs, durable formats, minimal dependencies, transmissible documentation. The disciplines are written down so they can outlive any particular steward.
